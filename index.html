Charles Babbage

The man whom many consider as the father of Computer

A time line of the life of Charles Babbage

Here is a time line of the life of Charles Babbage

  • 1791 - Born in London, England
  • 1808 - Moved into the old Rowdens house in East Teignmouth
  • 1812 - Formed the Analytical Society with his friends. He was tranferrd to Peterhouse, Cambridge. Despite being the top mathematician there he did not graduate with honours.
  • 1814 - Received a degree without examination. He married Georgiana Whitmore.
  • 1815 - Lectured on astronomy to the Royal Institutin
  • 1816 - Elected as a Fellow of the Royal Society. He was a candidate for a job in teaching at Haileybury College with having recommendations from James Ivory and John Playfair, but lost to Hery Walter.
  • 1819 - At the University of Edinburgh, applied to be a professor with the recommendations of Pierre Laplace but William Wallace got the post.
  • 1821 - Started work on the Difference Engine
  • 1822 - Articulates his idea for a calculating engine
  • 1823 - Government granted him £1500 to construct the Difference Engine.
  • 1826 - Bought the unfinished actuarial tables of George Barrett and surveyed the field in Comparative View of the Various Institutions for the Assurance of Lives.
  • 1827 - He inherited a large estate on his father's death. His wife and a son also passed away.
  • 1828 - After failing to obtain the position of pofessor in Cambridge for three times (in 1820, 1823 and 1826) he finally became the professor there.
  • 1831 - Founded the British Association for the Advancement of Science
  • 1832 - Joseph Clement, one of Babbage's engineer completed a small portion of Difference Engine. British government suspended funding for the Difference Engine. His book Economy of Mancufactures and Machinery was published.
  • 1833 - Started work on the Analytical Engine, an engine that was intended to perform any mathematical task. Augusta Ada Byron (yet to be Ada Lovelace), begins documentation on his Calculating machines.
  • 1834 - Statistical Society was founded. Backed by Richard Jones and Robert Malthus, he was its public face.
  • 1837 - He designed the Analytical Engine, the first general-purpose computer.
  • 1838 - Invented the pilot (also called a cow-catcher), the metal frame attached to the front of locomotives that clears the tracks of obstacles.
  • 1843 - His friend Ada Lovelace described in the Notes how the Analytical Engine could be programmed
  • 1871 - Died at the age of 79 in Marylebone, London, England
